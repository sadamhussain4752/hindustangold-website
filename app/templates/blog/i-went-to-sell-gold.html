Disadvantages of Pledging the Gold

There are several potential disadvantages of pledging gold as collateral for a loan or other financial arrangement:

Risk of Forfeiture: If the borrower is unable to repay the loan, the lender may seize the pledged gold as collateral. This means the borrower would lose their gold.

Loss of Liquidity: Gold is considered a liquid asset, meaning it can be easily bought or sold. Pledging gold as collateral reduces its liquidity, as the borrower may not be able to access it or sell it until the loan is repaid.

Storage & Insurance Costs: The borrower may need to store the pledged gold in a safe place and pay for insurance to protect it from theft or damage.

Reduced Potential For Appreciation: If the price of gold increases, the borrower would miss out on that appreciation, as the pledged gold is not accessible to them.

Interest Rate Risk: Pledging gold at a fixed rate of interest for a longer period could lead to a mismatch between the interest rate of the loan and the return from gold investment.

Limited Use Of The Asset: During the pledge period, the borrower is unable to use the gold for personal use or to sell it for cash.
