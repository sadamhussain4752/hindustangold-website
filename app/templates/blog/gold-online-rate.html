24 Karat Gold has higher selling price than 22 Karat Gold

Compared to 22 Karat gold, which has other metals mixed in with it, 24 Karat gold is purer and has more gold per unit of weight. Because of its greater gold content, 24 karat gold is more valuable and hence more expensive.

Additionally, 24 Karat gold is softer and more susceptible to scratches and other damage compared to 22 Karat gold, which has a stronger and more durable composition due to the presence of other metals. These factors contribute to the price difference between 24 Karat and 22 Karat gold.

Gold prices are determined by several factors.

Supply & Demand Dynamics: Changes in the demand for gold can drive prices up or down, as can changes in the supply of gold from mining and recycling.

Interest Rates: Higher Interest Rates can make bonds and other fixed-income investments more attractive to investors, reducing demand for gold and driving prices down.

Inflation Expectations: Gold is often seen as a hedge against inflation, so when inflation expectations rise, demand for gold may increase and prices may go up.

Political & Economic Stability: Demand for gold may increase as a result of political and economic unrest as investors look for a safe haven for their holdings.

Currency Fluctuations: Gold is often priced in US dollars, so fluctuations in the value of the dollar can impact gold prices.

Mining Costs: The cost of gold mining can affect the price since manufacturers may raise or lower their pricing to pay expenses or boost profits.

These are just a few of the many factors that can influence the price of gold, and the importance of each factor can vary over time.

Why Physical Gold is safer than the Digital Gold?

Physical gold is often considered safer than digital gold because it is a tangible asset that can be stored and physically held. This can provide peace of mind and a sense of security for investors, as they have direct control over their investment and can avoid the risks associated with digital assets such as cyber threats, hacking, and technical issues.

Moreover, physical gold has a long history of being a safe-haven asset and has maintained its value over time, even during periods of economic and political instability. This stability can provide a level of security that is not always present in the digital world.
